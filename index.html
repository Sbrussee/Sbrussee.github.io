About Me

I am currently a PhD student in Computational Pathology at the Leiden University Medical Center (LUMC). My research interests lie at the intersection of topological- or graph-based deep learning and pathology. With a strong background in Bioinformatics and Computer Science, I am passionate about leveraging advanced computational techniques to advance our understanding of complex biological systems and improve diagnostic processes in pathology.

My goal is to combine graph neural networks, computational topology, and graph signal processing techniques with biomedical image analysis to advance research in rare skin diseases and computational pathology as a whole. Through my work, I aim to contribute to the development of innovative solutions that can enhance patient care and advance the field of pathology.
